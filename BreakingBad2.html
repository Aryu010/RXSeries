Sinopsi and Information

    Highly acclaimed by critics, this television drama written and produced by Vince Gilligan ('The X-Files') shows the life of Walter White (Bryan Cranston 'Malcolm'), a genius in the field of chemistry whose existence is marked by enormous frustration both at a personal and professional level.

    Embittered by a monotonous and dull relationship with his wife and unable to prove his brilliance by working as a high school teacher, Walter takes a radical turn in his way of life when he discovers that he has terminal cancer. From then on, in his own way, he decides to reaffirm his love for his family and for chemistry by setting up a methamphetamine lab alongside Jesse Pinkman (Aaron Paul), an old and troubled student, in order to leave his wife and son in good stead when he is gone.

    The series explores the limits of being human and exposes Walter to situations he could never have imagined being in. For a long time, the series shows this chemistry professor finding himself in these kinds of situations, which takes him straight back to his former lifestyle.     Seasons

      First season: Walter White decides to enter the world of illegal drugs after being diagnosed with lung cancer. As a chemistry teacher, he uses his knowledge and skills to make methamphetamine alongside a former student, Jesse Pinkman (Aaron Paul). During their first days as drug producers, they have several problems with local distributors. In addition, they begin to be famous, thanks to the drug they produce, and expand their territory. Walt creates a nickname, Heisenberg, to make himself known in his second life.

      Second season: Walter decides to continue with the business despite all the conflicts it entails. In addition, he is forced to hire the services of a dishonest lawyer, Saul Goodman, to help the trafficker Badger after he is arrested by the police. Blego, another of the distributors, is murdered by a rival gang. While all this is going on, Jesse begins a relationship with Jane, a young woman who is recovering from her drug addiction, and Walt finds a new partner named Gus. At the end of the season, Walter's wife begins to suspect about her husband's second life.

      Third season: In this season, Walter decides to confess everything to his wife to relieve the tension between them. However, Skyler is surprised and demands a formal divorce. However, Gus offers her a full laboratory and a brilliant assistant, Gale. In addition, Jesse learns that Gus has ordered to kill Combo and wants to get revenge. Gus begins to lose confidence in Walter and orders his assassination. Upon his return, Walt asks Jesse to assassinate Gale to ensure the survival of both of them.

      Season 4: After Gale's murder, Gus tightens security at the lab and tries to fragment the friendship between Jesse and Walter. While Jesse works with one of Gus's droughts, Walter stays in the lab preparing the methamphetamine. Both protagonists join forces and decide to assassinate their boss. To do so, they convince a criminal from the cartel to explode a suicide bomb. Later, Walt and Jesse destroy the laboratory.

      Season Five: After Gus' murder, Walter and Jesse re-establish methamphetamine production and distribution. They are joined by Mike Ehrmantraut, a former henchman of Gus. Saul puts them in contact with a team of house fumigators to cook the methamphetamine in the houses. Walt kills Mike because he has the DEA behind him and jeopardizes the business. He also hires a gang of neo-Nazis to assassinate the informants in prison.
